Graphic Design

Several years ago, before I knew of the industry of UX Design, I was very interested in graphic design. Informally, I have been drawn to it since I was a kid. As an adult, I continued to explore my interest through personal design projects.

Although I loved studying visual design at UC Berkeley Extension’s Professional Program in Graphic Design, I was concerned enough about earning potential to support my family in the SF Bay Area that I decided not to pursue a career shift into Graphic Design 10 years ago. At that time, I also wasn’t aware of the new UX Design field.

LEAN Manufacturing

After studying graphic design, but before learning of UX Design, I studied and became certified to teach and train employees in Quality Assurance (LEAN) implementation through periodic process improvement review.

Also referred to as The Toyota Way these principles and behaviors value continuous process improvement, while respecting people working in the process. In other words, without directly stating it, The Toyota Way places a high value on the user experiences of those working on the production line/process.

Art (Highbrow & Low)

I have a love affair with modern art. My first crush was on Rothko’s relationships with colors. I’ve grown to learn and be inspired by many since then. The Pop Art movement has a special place for the social commentary wrapped in humor.

Some of my favorite early drawings (let’s call them lowbrow art) were from Mad magazine. In a similiar spirit as Mad, my friends’ and I also avidly collected Wacky Packages... those cynical cartoons mocking modern consumer products/culture. Given all this, how could I not be interested in design? No, seriously...how?
