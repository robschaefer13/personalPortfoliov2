Why UX Design?

Graphic Design

Several years ago, before I knew of the industry of UX Design, I was very interested in graphic design. Informally, I have been drawn to it since I was a kid. As an adult, I continued to explore my interest through personal design projects. Later, after several successful and fun graphic design courses through UC Berkeley, I realized that I wanted a career with better earning potential so that I could best support a family in the SF Bay Area. I also didn’t know that conceptual and process design could be intertwined with graphic design!

LEAN Manufacturing

After studying graphic design, but before learning of UX Design, I studied and became certified to teach and train employees in Quality Assurance (LEAN) implementation through periodic process improvement review.

Also referred to as “The Toyota Way” these principles and behaviors value continuous process improvement, while respecting people working in the process. In other words, without directly stating it, “The Toyota Way” places a high value on the user experiences of those working on the production line/process.

Personal User Experiences

Add to that, my personal user experiences continued to fuel my: “Why is it this way now?”, and “Can’t this be better?” questions, which is the essence of UX Design.

On a fairly regular basis I find myself in personal situations dealing with the frustration of poor design.

Situations like:

  • When, as a tall man, I sit in an airplane seat and the person sitting in front of me reclines into my knees.
  • When typeface on a prescription label is too small to read.
  • When my five-year-old son can’t reach the bathroom sink to wash his hands.

Art (Highbrow & Low)

I have a love affair with modern art. My first crush was on Rothko’s relationships with colors. I’ve grown to learn and be inspired by many since then. The Pop Art movement has a special place for the social commentary wrapped in humor. Some of my favorite early drawings (let’s call them lowbrow art) were from Mad magazine. In a similiar spirit as Mad, my friends’ and I also avidly collected Wacky Packages, those cynical cartoons mocking modern consumer products. Given all this, how could I not be interested in design? No, seriously... how?
